1. How is my grade determined?

PTPS can only be taken on a pass/no pass basis, and you need 70% to pass.

There are four main requirements:

1. Attending DeCal

2. Attending your class

3. CSPIIJ

4. Turning in your Reader at the end of the semester which contains all your lesson plans and student attendance records.

2. Does PTPS require a minimum SAT score to become a teacher?

No! In our experience, our teachers’ SAT scores have not had an effect on their teaching effectiveness. What is more important is a teacher’s passion and ability to connect with their students.

There are a multitude of reasons why you should teach, regardless of your SAT score. We serve a wide range of high school students with initial scores ranging from below 1500 to over 2200. High school students appreciate teachers with similar backgrounds. We also provide you with all the teaching materials you need to review the material you will be teaching beforehand.

3.What do PTPS teachers go on to do?

Many of our teachers go on to other paid tutoring/teaching jobs, and PTPS teachers are actively recruited by organizations like Teach for America, CollegeSpring, Aspire Education, and Breakthrough Collaborative for full-time and summer jobs. PTPS teachers and coordinators use their PTPS experience to get involved in other organizations and companies, such as SLC tutoring, private tutoring, working in other non-profits, working in for-profit companies, and becoming undergraduate student instructors (UGSIs).

4. How can I become a coordinator (coord)?

Every semester, anyone can apply for any position. We look for applicants who work well with others, can make the time commitment to PTPS, and have the motivation and drive to implement new initiatives to better the program.

4.Who will I be teaching with?

You will have a teaching partner. However, due to scheduling conflicts or a teacher’s personal preference, it is possible to teach alone.

5.Do I need to teach math and verbal?

No, you will be teaching math or verbal, based on the preferences you stated in your application.

6.Do I need to make my own lesson plans?

No, we provide you with all the curriculum resources needed. However, you are free to run your class as you see fit.

7.What is CSPIIJ?

Each PTPS teacher is expected to contribute an extra two hours of CSPIIJ. This usually entails leading a workshop.

8.How much of a time commitment is PTPS?

It is usually a 4 to 5 hour weekly commitment, depending on your transit time. Your transit time is dependent on the preferences you stated on your teacher application and the schedule of each site. CSPIIJ is an additional 2 hours, and grading at the beginning and end of the semester necessitates additional time. A few sites require fingerprinting and TB testing. You will have time to lesson plan with your teaching partner during DeCal.

9.Will I be compensated for any expenses?

Yes, you are compensated for BART travel costs. Your AC Transit Class Pass allows you to ride all AC Transit buses for free. If your site requires fingerprinting or TB testing, you will also be compensated for that.

10.Can I teach at more than one site?

Yes, and if you do so, you can take PTPS as a three-unit class.

11.What do the coordinators do?

PTPS Coordinator Positions and Responsibilities

All Coordinators

• Attend EVERY decal – no exceptions on what type of coordinator. If you have to miss, you must contact the Executive Director beforehand.

• Provide a thorough update on tasks at hand during each board meeting.

• Outline goals to achieve with measurable results each semester and share them during meetings.

• Other coordinator responsibilities outlined during mandatory all-day coordinator meeting.

• Attend all-day coordinator meeting.

Executive Director - Admin

• Acts as head liaison and representative of the entire group

• Oversees all administrative aspects, including budget, finance, room allocations, planning, calendaring, teacher and student issues, etc.

• Completes all administrative forms and reports

• Plans curriculum and oversees all teachers

• Responsible for completing (or delegating the completion of) all tasks not specifically allocated to another coordinator.

• Maintain itemized ledger of all PTPS accounts, including all income and expenses

• Maintain a full year budget sheet with all expenses and income

• Prepare semester ASUC budget report and annual Cal Corps Progress Report

Executive Director - Decal

• Acts as head liaison and representative of the entire group

• Plans curriculum and oversees all teachers

• Coordinates printing of all documents

Site Coordinator

• Establish contacts with local high schools and community centers

• Develop and plan PTPS course locations and research potential new sites

• Ensure sites have adequate materials (board, markers, chairs, etc.)

• Prepare site transportation information for teachers

• Ensures adequate enrollment at all sites

• Note teachers who miss their teaching class at their site and for finding substitutes

• Oversea site splits and ask crucial questions to ensure that sites are stocked properly and attendance is stable

Internal Publicity Coordinator

• Coordinate semester recruitment of new teachers

• Handle all program reimbursements

• Handle all program purchases

• Explore fundraising possibilities and prepare grant drafts (ASUC EEF, PSF)

• Coordinate fundraising events

• Creates testimonies of teachers

• Coordinates fliering and advertising for new teachers

External Publicity Coordinator

• Publicize SAT classes to students at sites

• Publicize all PTPS outreach, educational, and workshop events to local area and community to ensure turnout

• Administer and update PTPS website

• Ensure that online registration is functioning and assessable for all sites.

• Handles collection of statistics to use for impact of PTPS

Verbal Coordinator

• Plans and teaches Verbal section of De-Cal, including providing additional Reading and teaching resources and materials including

potential exercises, worksheets and homework

• Create verbal lesson plans

• Prepares the Verbal section of the teacher manual and student workbook in conjunction with the Math Coordinators

• Oversees all Verbal Teachers

Math Coordinator

• Plans and teaches Math section of De-Cal, including providing additional Math and teaching resources and materials including potential

exercises, worksheets and homework

• Create math lesson plans

• Prepares the Math section of the teacher manual and student workbook in conjunction with the Verbal Coordinators

• Oversees all Math Teachers

Workshops Coordinator

• Plan and conduct workshops on College Admissions, SAT Subject Exams, Essay writing, and other topics relevant to our students

• Plan the Mock SAT exam

• Recruit and train teachers (out of current and past teacher pool) to help lead workshops

• Prepare curriculum for all planned workshops

• Oversee all teachers in workshops
